Analysis on World Population In The Year 2020

This analysis tells us certain aspects thats going on around the world during the year 2020. The analysis contains 143 countries In this analysis, we will be looking into the population in each country, the growth % from year 2019 to 2020, the GDP in each country and how the GDP affects the life expectancy of the residents Hopefully, this analysis would help the reader have a better understanding on whats going on around the world.

World population in the year 2020


This visualization shows the population of the world in the year 2020. It uses colour staturation to determine the density of the country, the darker the country means that the higher the population. Some countries did not include their data, therefore the countries without data can be seen having a while hue. From the observation, the countries with high population include China, United States, Russia, India and Brazil. While the country that we can see having the least amount of peopl is Greenland

Country growth from year 2019 to 2020


This visualizaion tells us the percentage (%) in growth in the countries from year 2019 to 2020. Countries with a positive growth would have a positive bar while countries with a negative growth will have a negative bar. From this visualizaion, the country with the highest growth % is Oman while the country with the highest negative growth is Lalvia

Country GDP in the year 2020


This visualizaion tells us the GDP per captia for every country in the year 2020, similar to the visualizaion about world population, it uses colour saturation to tell us the country's GDP. The greener the country means that the higher the GPD, in this case the country with the highest GPD is Australia and most parts of North America.

The life expectancy against country GPD in 2020


This last visualizaion is a scatterplot showing how the country's GDP affects the life expectancy of their citizens. We can see that there is a relationship between these variables as the higher the GDP, the higher the life expectancy. This makes sense as the higher GPD countries would supply more healthcare to their people thus increasing their life expectancy. The country with the highest GPD and life expectancy is Luxembourg
